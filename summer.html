Summer Internship Program

Summer Internship Program

A Summer Internship Programme (SIP) has been organized for the undergraduate 2nd and 3rd year students for providing them exposure and practical experience to the facilities available in the BIT AICTE IDEA LAB. The SIP brochure was circulated in 2nd week of April and the students were asked to fill the online registration form, there was a huge response and within three days the limit of maximum number of participants was reached. A total of 102 students registered for the programme. The event started on 2nd May with the inaugural speech by Dr. Arun Arora, Chief Mentor (BIT AICTE IDEA LAB). In his speech he motivated the participants to apply the classroom theory to practice and get trained in latest technologies, various equipments and facilities available in the lab. During the inaugural function Dr. Mohan Kumar Gupta, Coordinator (BIT AICTE IDEA LAB), Co-coordinator Dr. Pawan Kumar Patnaik, four IDEA LAB Gurus, faculty members and staff were present. The inaugural function was followed by the offline registration of students and the schedule of the SIP was communicated to them along with the allotment of Intern ID to them. All the registered participants were divided in to four groups each having 25 students in it. In the first week from 2nd to 6th May during first half (10am to 1:00pm), the first group (Intern ID 1 to 25) was trained on “Laser Cutting Machine, Vinyl Cutter and Sublimation Printing by Dr. Santosh Mishra (IDEA LAB GURU). Second group (Intern ID 25 to 50) was trained on “3D Printing and Scanning” by Dr. Anil Kumar (IDEA LAB GURU), Third group (Intern ID 51 to 75) was trained on “PCB Designing using EAGLE CAD and PCB Fabrication” by Prof. Suchitra Pandey (IDEA LAB GURU) and the fourth group (Intern ID 76 to 100) was introduced to “IOT Sensors and its applications” and trained on “Programming of various Development boards like ARDUINO UNO and ESP32” by Prof. Kauleshwar Prasad (IDEA LAB GURU). On 2nd and 3rd May during the second half (2:00 pm to 5:00 pm), project allotment and group formation was done. 17 real-time projects were allotted to the groups, each group was assigned a faculty mentor and a project leader (Student Ambassador). The project groups started working on their projects from 4th May itself. During the 2nd, 3rd, and 4th week in first half (10am to 1:00pm) all the four groups were trained in the above-mentioned areas by the IDEA LAB GURUs and in the second half, the project groups worked on their projects. All the interns actively worked on their projects and tried to incorporate the training concepts practically in their projects. In the last week, Project exhibition was done and our Chief Mentor, Coordinator, Co-Coordinator and Lab Gurus evaluated the projects and identified some of the unique projects that can be applied for patent. Assessment of the interns was also done through quiz and the interns were required score more than 60% marks in it and were also required to maintain a minimum of 80% attendance during the SIP. The interns prepared a training report and submitted on the last day of the internship programme. The certificate was provided to only the interns who fulfilled the above-mentioned criteria. Feedback from the Interns: The Interns felt very enthusiastic to learn that the advanced facilities have been established in our institute which will provide practical learning experience in the field of smart and intelligent manufacturing. The feedback form was circulated among the students and few words from the participants are: The most significant accomplishment or satisfying moment of the Internship Programme was, “Experiencing laser cutting and engraving, Collaborating with the team members”, “When we finally completed our project & make it presentable on exhibition & when our respected Chief Mentor Sir, Coordinator Sir & all our faculty members praise our project”, “It improved leadership quality as well as have gained a lot of confidence”, “In this internship we learnt a lot and actually converted theory into practical that was most satisfying part”.
